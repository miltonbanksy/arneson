By Matt Jackson. Game like it's 1972

CHARACTER CREATION

  1. Write down a Name, be evocative and be creative. Namescan implyTraits.
  2. Write down a few words or phrases, called Traits, about your character, the referee will approve or suggest changes. Traits should be specific and not overly general.
  3. Write down any Gear you might be carrying. Gear should be specific items that may help with the quest.
  4. Your character has 4 Hit Points. The referee may award more if your Traits suggest it.

TESTS

When resolutions are not clear, the referee will call for a test. Players will roll 2d6:

If one of your Traits can help, roll 3d6, but keep only the highest two dice rolled. If a trait could hinder you, roll 3d6 but keep only the two lowest rolled dice.

  • High roll = successful, good results.
  • Middling roll = does not change the situation, or negotiated /mixed results (fleeting success, success with a downside, failure with an upside).
  • Low roll = unsuccessful, bad result

OPPOSED & COMBAT

Both combatants roll 2d6 (or 3d6, keep in mind Traits). The side with the high result is successful. If a tie, we negotiate. The difference in rolls is the damage or effectiveness of the success.

GEAR

Some Gear provides a +1 to rolls. Some Gear causes more damage or may have effects outside of hit points. Armor grants extra hit points. The referee has the final say on this type of ruling.

ADVANCEMENT

The referee will level the characters as they progress through the adventures. Upon leveling, the player will raise their character’s HP by 2 or add a Trait. New Traits should be a reflection of the narrative of the game.
